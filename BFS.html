Business Financial Services

(3PS and Annual Basis Services)

We advice to clients regarding money management. The role involves thorough research of the marketplace to find the best products and services available for your clients’ needs. Once you have found what you’re looking for, the next step is to convince your clients that these products/services are what they need to grow their wealth.

The items you sell depend on your clients but may include employee pension schemes for companies or pension, investment or mortgage advice to private clients. If you enjoy navigating the minefield that is the financial market, read on to discover how to become a financial services advisor.

Restricted or Independent Financial Services Advisor (FSA):

  • Restricted: We will focus on a small number of products from a limited number of providers and attempt to sell these to your clients.
  • Independent: We will research all financial products and services available on the marketplace and must provide clients with unbiased advice.

Our role will include:

  • Meet with clients in order to understand their needs and desires.
  • Conduct in-depth reviews of your clients’ financial circumstances.
  • Complete risk analyses.
  • Research the financial marketplace and inform clients of the existing range of products and services available.
  • Prepare financial plans with a mixture of long and short term investments.
  • Fully implement the plans approved by your clients.
  • Provide clients with updates on the performances of their portfolios.
  • Promote and sell financial products with a view to meeting specified targets.
  • Negotiate with suppliers in order to secure the best rates for your clients.
  • Ensure you remain up to date with the latest financial legislation and newest products on the market.
